* You MUST use at least one of the below datasets (Scripps or non-Scripps) if you are doing a software-based project.   
* You MUST specifically use one of the Scripps datasets to be eligible for the $1500 Scripps Challenge.

**Scripps Data**

**Geophysics Data:**

- [Ground Velocity from Earthquake Simulations](https://zenodo.org/records/8170242): This dataset contains simulated ground motion maps generated using physics-based models for Southern California. It provides high-resolution snapshots of seismic wave propagation to help predict how different areas react during an earthquake.  
  * Associated Paper: [Rekoske et al. - 2023 - Instantaneous Physics-Based Ground Motion Maps Usi.pdf](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1giQgJzDm7EPH9gezjNY3tJpigIvPn-WW/view?usp=sharing)  
- [Simulated Earthquake Seismograms From Southern California Earthquakes](https://zenodo.org/records/12520845): This collection includes thousands of synthetic seismograms created through reduced-order modeling. It is designed to provide a large-scale training set for machine learning applications in seismology and structural engineering.  
  * Associated Paper:  
    [Rekoske et al. - 2025 - Reduced-order modelling for complex three-dimensio.pdf](https://drive.google.com/file/d/19OEDraoZJUsplvdEyiF8la1Cs3LGYp8J/view?usp=sharing)

**Oceanography:**

- [EasyOneArgo Data](https://www.seanoe.org/data/00961/107233/): This global array consists of thousands of autonomous drifting floats that measure ocean temperature and salinity. The data helps scientists track heat storage and cycle changes in the upper 2,000 meters of the ocean.  
  * [Data Specification](https://archimer.ifremer.fr/doc/00960/107206/120355.pdf)  
  * Data Product Overview: [OSM\_poster\_awongetal2026.pdf](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1RJeJdU-aZt7dXp3zIWVGvSf20AJRvX2Y/view?usp=drive_link)  
- [CalCOFI Data Portal](https://calcofi.org/data/): The California Cooperative Oceanic Fisheries Investigations provides long-term records of the physical and biological state of the California Current. It includes decades of data on ocean chemistry, plankton populations, and fish larvae. It contains 11 datasets across oceanography and marine ecosystems.pol  
- [Sea Surface Height Data for the Gulf of Mexico:](http://ecco.ucsd.edu/DATA/GOM/run2_clim_v2_ssh.nc) Sea surface height data sampled every day from a 40-year long numerical model simulation, forced with constant boundary conditions at the open boundary (north & east).  
  * [Visualization Movie](https://ecco.ucsd.edu/DATA/GOM/run2_clim_ssh.mp4)  
  * [gulf\_of\_mexico\_hackathon\_data\_SIO.pptx](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1uosASLL0uCcO0TbNw0YBXCnY5rmtOd5i/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=117305649457083873974&rtpof=true&sd=true)  
- [Spray Data](https://spraydata.ucsd.edu/data-access): This database hosts information collected by underwater Spray gliders that patrol the California coast. The gliders measure ocean currents and water properties along repeated tracks to monitor seasonal and yearly changes. It contains data for 15+ projects.  
- [California Current Ecosystem (CCE) Mooring Data](https://mooring.ucsd.edu/cce/): This site provides real-time and archived data from stationary moorings located in the California Current Ecosystem. These sensors track surface and subsurface conditions, including ocean acidification markers and meteorological data.

**Surface:**

- [Heat Map Data](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/155TtgXJj_2SedORb1f8cDM_b4puNRluL): A miniaturized mobile weather station has been developed to map the variations in temperature and relative humidity throughout the UCSD campus, enabling novel research in urban heat, climate resilience for public health  
  * [DataHacks Heat Mapping Data Overview](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1JkAcn3qI3XQbTla1LpreFpmWZQlbu7DfVFeCnBKtkuA/edit?tab=t.0)

**Species:**

- [iNaturalist Species Data](https://www.inaturalist.org/observations): This website is a global social network and database where users share observations of plants, animals, and fungi. It uses crowdsourced identification and image recognition technology to record biodiversity and provide open-access data for scientific research and conservation.  
  * Anyone with a free account can export data from iNaturalist as a spreadsheet in csv format.   
  *  [How to Search iNaturalist Observations](https://help.inaturalist.org/en/support/solutions/articles/151000169670)

**Non-Scripps Data** 

**Environmental**

- [NASA National Snow and Ice Data Center](https://nsidc.org/home): This center archives data on the Earth’s cryosphere, covering glaciers, ice sheets, and sea ice. Researchers use this information to study climate change impacts on frozen regions and global sea-level rise.  
- [US Environmental Protection Agency](https://www.epa.gov/data): Central portal for environmental data managed by the US Environmental Protection Agency, organized by topics such as air quality, chemical usage, and water safety. It provides access to public datasets, geospatial mapping tools, and the Toxics Release Inventory to help users monitor pollution and public health factors in specific locations.  
- [US Department of Commerce Environmental Data](https://www.commerce.gov/data-and-reports/environmental-data): From daily weather forecasts, severe storm warnings, and climate monitoring to fisheries management, coastal restoration and supporting marine commerce, NOAA’s products and services support economic vitality and affect more than one-third of America’s gross domestic product. 

**Energy**

- [DNV Energy Transition Outlook 2025 (Energy)](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1z70CN4CsyzpUS6CN7NvTC9Z7ZZtw6QR6/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=113914661221302710293&rtpof=true&sd=true) \- Projections from 2024 to 2060 for primary energy supply and demand across the transport, building, and manufacturing sectors. It tracks the growth of renewable energy, nuclear power, and electrification alongside the production levels of fossil fuels. The data also includes economic metrics for hydrogen, carbon pricing, and grid financing, as well as modeling for population and GDP trends.  
- [Solar Power Data (ZenPower)](https://github.com/Zen-Power-Solar/DataHacks-ZenPower-Challenge-Spring-2026/tree/main) \- These datasets consists of solar installation permit records and related information. It tracks system capacities, geographic locations, and processing timelines for residential and commercial solar projects. More information can be found in the GitHub Repository.  
- [US Energy Information Administration](https://www.eia.gov/opendata/): U.S. Energy Information Administration's comprehensive collection of time-series energy data through a public API and open data tools. It covers a wide range of categories, including production and consumption metrics for electricity, petroleum, and natural gas, as well as international energy trends and CO2 emissions projections.  
  * [API Documentation](https://www.eia.gov/opendata/documentation.php) 

**Oceanography**

- [Monterrey Bay Aquarium Research Institute Data](https://www.mbari.org/our-work/data-repositories/): This website serves as a central hub for the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute's (MBARI) diverse oceanographic data. It provides access to a wide range of repositories, including real-time mooring observations, deep-sea biological archives, seafloor mapping, and chemical data from autonomous profiling floats.

**Food Waste**

* [U.S. Municipal Solid Waste](https://datadryad.org/dataset/doi:10.5061/dryad.bzkh189h4): This dataset contains annual municipal solid waste records for 36 US states covering the years 1996 through 2019\. It was compiled to evaluate the effectiveness of state-level food waste bans by tracking aggregate waste volumes and disposal trends across different jurisdictions.

**Rare Disease & Genetic Variants**

- [1000 Genomes Project](https://www.internationalgenome.org/data/): Population-scale variant data across diverse human populations. Standard reference for population genetics.  
  * Use Case: Population stratification, ancestry correction, variant frequency analysis  
- [ClinVar](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/clinvar/): NCBI’s database of variant-to-disease pathogenicity annotations curated from clinical submissions worldwide.  
  * Use Case: Rare disease subtyping, variant classification, PolyQ and neurodegenerative disease variant analysis  
- [gnomAD](https://gnomad.broadinstitute.org/): Population-scale variant frequencies across 125,000+ exomes and 15,000+ genomes. Essential for filtering common variants.  
  * Use Case: Rare variant prioritization, allele frequency filtering, population-aware variant calling

**Cancer Genomics**

- [TCGA (subset)](https://portal.gdc.cancer.gov/): The Cancer Genome Atlas: gene expression, somatic mutations, copy number, and survival data across 33 cancer types.  
  * Use Case: Cancer biomarker discovery, subtype classification, survival prediction  
- [CCLE](https://depmap.org/portal/ccle/): Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia: gene expression and drug response data across 1,000+ cancer cell lines.  
  * Use Case: Cancer biomarker discovery, subtype classification, survival predictionDrug sensitivity prediction, cancer dependency mapping, multi-omics integration

**Polygenic Traits & GWAS**

- [GWAS Catalog](https://www.ebi.ac.uk/gwas/): EBI’s curated database of genome-wide association study summary statistics across thousands of traits and diseases.  
  * Use Case: Polygenic risk scoring, variant prioritization, trait architecture analysis  
- [FinnGen](https://www.finngen.fi/en/access_results): Large-scale Finnish biobank with public GWAS summary statistics across 2,000+ disease endpoints.  
  * Use Case: Cross-population PRS, disease risk modeling, phenome-wide association studies

**Single-Cell & Protein Databases**

- [CELLxGENE Census](https://chanzuckerberg.github.io/cellxgene-census/): Chan Zuckerberg Initiative resource with single-cell RNA-seq data from millions of human and mouse cells across hundreds of tissues.  
  * Use Case: Cell type classification, gene regulatory networks, perturbation biology  
- [UniProt](https://www.uniprot.org/): World’s largest annotated protein sequence database (250M+ sequences). Training data for ESM-2 and Amplify.  
  * Use Case: Protein function prediction, sequence embedding, protein engineering  
- [10x Genomics Datasets](https://www.10xgenomics.com/datasets): Reference single-cell RNA-seq datasets from 10x Chromium platforms, widely used for benchmarking.  
  * Use Case: Single-cell preprocessing, clustering benchmarks, spatial transcriptomics

**Market Data – Economic Track**

- [Polymarket Binary Prediction Market Data](https://github.com/austntatious/DATAHACKS2026/tree/main): Multi-source financial dataset combining Polymarket binary prediction markets (YES/NO on BTC/ETH/SOL price movements), full depth-of-book order book snapshots, Binance spot 10-level LOB data, and Chainlink oracle settlement prices. Sampled at the definition of 1 second: across 5m, 15m, and hourly intervals.  
  * Use Case: Market microstructure modeling, binary outcome modelling and prediction, research on cross-market signal alignment

**Clinical / Physiologic**

- [MIMIC-IV (subset)](https://physionet.org/content/mimiciv/3.1/): De-identified electronic health records from Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center. Requires credentialed access via PhysioNet.  
  * Use Case: Clinical outcome prediction, ICU risk modeling, EHR-based disease classification  
- [PhysioNet](https://physionet.org/): Repository of physiologic signals including ECG and ICU time-series datasets.  
  * Use Case: Physiologic signal classification, cardiac event prediction, ICU mortality modeling
