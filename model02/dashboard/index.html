Gulf of Maine Sea Level:
Observations, Drivers & Projections

An end-to-end analysis of relative sea level at Portland and Bar Harbor, Maine, integrating a century of tide-gauge observations with satellite altimetry, GRACE ocean mass, Greenland ice-sheet mass, EasyOneArgo (cleaned and normalized Argo float data), and the North Atlantic Oscillation. EasyOneArgo is used directly in model calibration and monthly forecasting through the retained density predictor and the overlap/regime filters that determine when a month is treated as constrained rather than extrapolative. We validate a ridge-regularized monthly reconstruction model, publish regime-labeled predictions through 2026, and project Portland annual-average sea level over the next century using a NAO-informed historical trend model.

01

Observed Sea Level

NOAA CO-OPS Tide Gauges

Monthly mean sea-level records for Portland (station 8418150, since 1912) and Bar Harbor (station 8413320, since 1947) were downloaded from the NOAA CO-OPS API, parsed into xarray datasets, and saved to NetCDF. Portland's century-long record anchors the annual projection track; Bar Harbor provides an independent modern cross-validation window.

Values are reported as monthly mean anomalies relative to each station's long-run mean, in meters. The upward trend visible from the 1960s onward reflects both global mean sea-level rise and local glacial isostatic adjustment (GIA) of approximately +1.1 mm/yr relative land motion at Portland.

A third station, Rockland (1970–1987), was collected but excluded from the validated monthly modeling window because the retained modern overlap dataset does not include a modern Rockland target series.

02

Climate Drivers

Sea level along the Maine coast integrates steric (density-driven), barystatic (ocean mass), and atmospheric forcing on interannual to decadal timescales. We assembled four physically distinct predictor families from satellite and in-situ archives. EasyOneArgo is highlighted explicitly because it is not just contextual oceanography: it is part of the calibrated monthly model and part of the forecasting regime logic.

North Atlantic Oscillation

The CPC monthly NAO index was fetched from NOAA and converted to a long artifact spanning 1950–2026. Annual means and winter DJF means were derived for use in the annual projection track. The NAO modulates atmospheric pressure gradients and wind-driven coastal sea-level anomalies across the Northwest Atlantic; it is the longest retained predictor family in the Sojs stack and the backbone of the century-scale projection.

Annual mean NAO index, 1950 – 2025

Greenland Ice Sheet Mass

The GRACE/GRACE-FO Greenland mascon time series was downloaded from PO.DAAC (GREENLAND_MASS_TELLUS_MASCON_CRI_TIME_SERIES_RL06.3_V4) and parsed from its HDR-delimited ASCII format (254 months, April 2002 – February 2026). Mass loss from Greenland contributes to global mean sea-level rise; its cumulative trend appears as a retained predictor in both the monthly and annual models, capturing barystatic sea-level contributions not resolved by regional altimetry alone.

Additional Predictor Families

Copernicus Marine SLA

Gulf of Maine area-mean sea-level anomaly from the DUACS L4 satellite altimetry product (cmems_obs-sl_glo_phy-ssh_my_allsat-l4-duacs-0.125deg_P1D, 1993–2024). This captures local steric and dynamic sea-level variability on timescales not directly visible in the tide gauge alone.

GRACE Ocean Mass

Historical (2002–2017) ocean bottom pressure anomaly from TELLUS_GRAC_L3_CSR_RL06_OCN_v04, reduced to a Gulf of Maine area-mean mass series. Continued post-2017 using GRACE-FO (TELLUS_GRFO_L3_CSR_RL06.3_OCN_v04), standardized onto the historical mean and standard deviation before insertion into the model.

EasyOneArgo / Argo Ocean Density

EasyOneArgo is the cleaned and normalized form of the underlying Argo float data used in Sojs. Monthly NW Atlantic shelf profiles were interpolated onto standard depth levels and aggregated to monthly 0–200 dbar shelf density. Only months with ≥18 profiles and ≥2 occupied grid cells are admitted. That support filter leaves 40 quality-controlled overlap months, defines the validated calibration window, and directly affects which forecast months can be labeled as observationally constrained.

03

Model Selection & Validation

Phase 4 Hindcast

Six candidate models were evaluated on the 40-month cleaned Argo-overlap window (July 2004 – June 2017) using 5-fold forward time-series cross-validation. Trend-adjusted seasonal cycles were removed from both target and predictors before cross-validation to isolate interannual model skill and prevent future data leakage.

The final selected model, ols_with_argo_ridge, uses ridge-regularized ordinary least squares with four predictors: Copernicus Gulf of Maine SLA, historical GRACE ocean mass, Greenland mass anomaly, and EasyOneArgo-derived 0–200 dbar shelf density. This Argo input is used in both calibration and forecasting, not just as a contextual side product. Argo density is preferred over separate temperature and salinity terms because density already encodes both physically, and including all three produced near-collinear columns that inflated coefficient variance on the short overlap window.

The model stores a reusable FinalModelFit object containing fitted target and predictor transforms, ridge coefficients, and station baseline metadata, enabling the prediction pipeline to reuse validated transforms without re-fitting.

Monthly Predictions (2004 – 2026)

The validated final model is applied across three distinct regimes, each labeled in the output product. Predictions span July 2004 through March 2026. The regime label is the most important metadata in the output — and EasyOneArgo support is one of the inputs that determines that label by controlling when the calibrated Argo density predictor is truly available.

05

Century-Scale Annual Projection

Portland Annual Sea Level, 2027 – 2126

A separate annual projection track was built specifically for Portland using the longest available predictor family — the monthly NAO record (1950–2026). Annual calendar-year means require at least 9 valid months per variable; the resulting annual table spans 1912 to 2026 with 113 Portland target years.

Five annual models were evaluated in a model ladder (baseline trend, trend+NAO, trend+NAO+SLA, trend+NAO+SLA+Greenland, and a ridge variant) using rolling 5-year and 10-year holdouts plus a final contiguous 10-year tail holdout. The selected model, trend_plus_nao, beats the baseline trend-only model on rolling 5-year holdouts (mean RMSE 0.037 m vs 0.049 m). Shorter-window models with SLA and Greenland do not yet generalize as well given the current data volume.

The century projection method extracts the historical annual trend from the training data, samples 4,000 bootstrap coefficient sets to represent trend uncertainty, and adds AR(1)-correlated residual noise learned from the fitted model (σ = 31.4 mm, ρ = 0.589). Deterministic land-motion reconstruction — GIA at +1.1 mm/yr — is added back to the simulated adjusted-target paths after simulation. The resulting fan shows annual-average absolute Portland sea level, not a physically forced climate scenario.

Key Limitations

  • Monthly predictions are not a closed sea-level budget.
  • Portland continuation regime (GRACE-FO) is materially weak: R² = −0.45 vs +0.61 for Bar Harbor.
  • Argo support filter creates real month-level gaps — regime coverage is intermittent, not continuous.
  • Historical GRACE ends in June 2017; any 2018+ constrained months require the GRACE-FO continuation.
  • The century projection extrapolates a historical trend; it is not a climate-scenario ensemble.
  • Land motion is first-pass GIA-only metadata (+1.1 mm/yr); no GPS-based VLM correction is applied.
  • Rockland is intentionally excluded — the retained modern overlap dataset does not validate a Rockland monthly window.

Methods Summary

  • Ridge OLS with 5-fold forward time-series cross-validation
  • EasyOneArgo / Argo density used in calibration, regime gating, and monthly forecasting
  • Predictor z-score normalization on training window means and σ
  • Trend-adjusted seasonal deseasonalization before cross-validation
  • Three-regime output labeling: constrained / continuation / extrapolation
  • Annual model ladder with rolling 5-year and 10-year holdouts
  • AR(1) residual noise structure for century projection simulation
  • 4,000-path bootstrap for projection uncertainty quantification
